# Distilled: Matt Barker — 30 Days of Proven LinkedIn Templates

Source: "30 Days of Proven LinkedIn Templates + Bonuses" (grew 0→50k followers, 12M views,
400+ inbound in 12 months). This file is the grounding corpus for prompt experiments.
Templates below are transcribed and grouped; first-person templates must be transformed to
educator voice unless the record has proof-bank facts (see plan §2).

## Core principles (from the pro tips)

1. Content for the target audience beats algorithm-hacking. Real problems, their words.
2. Hooks: "How to" = education promise. Numbers = engagement gold (hyperbole OK, clickbait not).
   Problem-callout hooks must be REAL problems the ICP has voiced.
3. Simplify the complex: bullet points make goals digestible/actionable/achievable.
4. Storytelling must be raw and vulnerable: current world → characters → conflict → solution.
5. Words that work: "steal", "simple", "easy", "cheat" (taboo/shortcut feel), "harsh truth"
   (fear + curiosity), "stop" (pattern interrupt).
6. Micro-commitment questions ("in 5 words or less…") make engagement feel like a game.
7. End list posts with "anything you'd add?" — near-impossible not to engage.
8. Old/new + before/after transformation posts are gold; timeline with specific details.
9. Nobody cares about you; they care about what you can do for them (Topic 101 pattern).
10. Format: short lines, no links in post, 3–5 hashtags (2022 advice — modern: 0–3), post
    consistently, reply to every comment in hour 1.

## The 30 templates (grouped into archetype families)

### A. Struggles → advice (Day 1)
"My X biggest struggles as a new [role] were: 1..N. [Callout to those in same position], here's
my advice: 1..N. What else do you struggle with?" — write as if talking to yourself 6 months ago.

### B. Routine / process transparency (Days 2, 9, 23)
- "My daily [topic] routine: [time]-[thing]… Total time - [x]."
- "This is how I get [desirable thing] in [short timeframe]: 1..3. I do this [frequency]."
- "My [topic] process from start to finish in X steps: 1..N. That's my process. What's yours?"

### C. How-to / education (Days 3, 10, 11, 16, 24, 28, 30)
- "How to become [desired thing]: - step - step - step. [Round-up]"
- "How to [dream outcome] in [timeframe]: - steps. [Advice to execute] [Potential outcome]"
- "[X] tips to [desired outcome] without [obstacle]: 1. [tip] [why it works] …" — obstacle must
  be the real reason they haven't done it; tip 1 should startle ("play dumb").
- "My [x] step framework that [positive outcome]: 1. [step]+context … What do you think?"
- "The best way to 10x your [topic]: [suggestion]. 3 tips: 1..3. [Actionable takeaway]"
- "Cheat [topic] by: - tactic - tactic - tactic. Use it how you want. Not how they want."
- "Simple [x] step trick to stop [real problem]: 1..3. [misconception twist]"

### D. Opinion / contrarian (Days 5, 6, 12, 19, 20, 21, 25)
- "[Topic] needs less of this: - dislikes. And more of: - likes."
- "[Topic] 101: Nobody cares about [thing]. They care about [thing]."
- "The worst thing you can do on [topic] is [very specific, very common negative thing]. If you
  are [negative outcome], stop. [Strong advice]"
- "Harsh truth: If you aren't [desired outcome], stop blaming [scapegoat]. Your [thing] is
  [real reason for failure]." — find what people moan about, show the real answer.
- "Your [thing] can be the [Ferrari/pop-culture] of [things] but if [specific part] is [bad]
  it's pointless."
- "Stop [topic problem]. I know [reason]. But you only need: - solution ×3"
- "99.9999% of [topic] I see doesn't address: - thing ×3. Way too much [x]. Not enough [y]."

### E. Lists / diagnostics (Days 7, 14, 17)
- "[X] things that destroy a [topic thing]: 1..N. Avoid at all costs. Anything you'd add?"
  (mistakes = inverted best practices)
- "[X] quick ways to not [common REAL problem]: 1..3. Your [thing] will improve once you stop."
- "Bad [topic]: - thing ×3. Good [topic]: - thing ×3. Anything you'd add?"

### F. Story / transformation (Days 8, 15, 22, 29) — PROOF-GATED (first-person facts)
- Journey: "[Timeframe] ago I [risk] with [further risk]. [Context] [Reason] [Obstacle] [Reality]…"
- Progress timeline: "[X] ago I [bad situation]. [X] ago I [change]… Now I have: - things.
  [x] things I didn't have [x] ago."
- Old vs new: "My old [topic]: ×3. My new [topic]: ×3. [topic] was [emotion]. I would [old
  result]. Now [new result]. Small change, big impact."
- How it started: "How it started as a [role]: - newbie things. [x] months later: - now things.
  [lesson] [inspiring takeaway]"

### G. Micro-engagement (Days 13, 26, 27)
- "In 5 words or less, what advice would you give [type of person]?"
- "[Type of person] are [adjective]. They aren't just [adj] at [main thing]. They're [adj] at:
  - underrated thing ×3. You can't JUST be a [main thing]."
- Dream/fun one-liner + image ("Is this what heaven feels like?") — skip for text automation.

### H. Method intro (Days 4, 18)
- "Worried your [thing] are [negative outcome]? Use this: [Name of method]: [explain]. A great
  way to [positive outcome]. Try this on your next [thing]. Any questions?"
- "Simple [topic] tip: [tip] [social proof] The most [metric] ones are usually [thing].
  Pro tip: [deeper tip within the tip]. What ya think?"

## Top hook patterns (from the 50-hook corpus)

- Steal: "Steal my [asset] for free (save this forever):" / "Steal this [x] (use it 100% of the time):"
- Save-time: "Save yourself [time] learning [topic] and steal my [n]-step [process] (I've
  [volume proof]):"
- This-is-how-I: "This is how I get [10 X] in [5 minutes] (for free):"
- Big number: "In [month] I generated [1,990,835 views]. Here are the [top 5]:" — PROOF-GATED
- Transformation: "[3 years] ago I was [specific bad details]." — PROOF-GATED
- How-to + parenthetical: "How to [outcome] in [n] steps (this is a secret):"
- Without-obstacle: "3 tips to [outcome] (without [expensive/slow obstacle]):"
- Micro-commitment: "In 5 words or less, [question]?"
- Needs-less: "[Topic] needs less of this:"
- Worried-problem: "Worried your [thing] is [feared outcome]? Use this:"
- Contrarian identity: "Getting [impressive metric] isn't a skill. [Underlying craft] is."
- Volume-lesson: "I [did X hundreds of times] in [timeframe]. If I lost my memory tomorrow,
  these are the [n] tips I'd write down:"

## Hook mechanics observed across the corpus

- Specific, odd numbers beat round ones (12.89 seconds, 1,990,835 views, £5,750.86).
- Parenthetical sweetener at the end of the hook: (for free) (save this) (this is a secret)
  (without spending £597 on a course) (ft. [credible name]).
- Front-load the outcome, back-load the effort/timeframe.
- One idea per line; the hook is its own line; blank line after the hook.
