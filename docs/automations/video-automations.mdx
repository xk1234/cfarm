> **WIP:** Video automations share the main Automations page and settings shell,
> but format-specific creation, generation, preview, and publishing support is
> not yet equally complete for every template.

Video automations appear alongside slideshows on the
[main Automations page](./index). Their cards show recent rendered-video
previews when available, connected accounts, schedule times, status, and an
Edit action.

![WIP video automation Overview using the shared vertical editor shell](/docs/automations/video-editor-overview.png)

## What exists

- A **Video** tab in the New automation catalog.
- Blank video automation creation and format presets.
- The shared vertical editor navigation: Overview, Video Format, Hooks & Voice,
  Schedule, Social Media Settings, and Settings.
- Video collection selection, generated-video viewing, account selection, and
  the shared scheduling/publishing configuration.
- Template-specific fields for media segments, copy, playback, audio, timing,
  and preview where the selected template implements them.

## Known video format concepts

Depending on the selected template, Video Format may include:

![WIP UGC video format editor with template, avatar collection, demo video, music, and preview](/docs/automations/video-editor-format.png)

- an avatar or source-video collection;
- one demo/reveal video that must play in full;
- video-collection segments that use clips or full playback;
- mutually exclusive Videos and Slides modes on supported segments;
- a slideshow automation, slide count, and seconds per slide in Slides mode;
- segment ordering and overlay copy; and
- soundtrack, timing, cuts, and preview controls.

Controls should only appear when the renderer consumes them. For example, a
full demo-video segment should not expose slideshow-style clip count or
seconds-per-clip settings.

## Still WIP

- Not every visible video template has the same end-to-end runner coverage.
- Required media preflight and empty states vary by template.
- Some templates still need stronger generated-output examples and benchmark
  documentation.
- Character/avatar training and arbitrary provider/model invocation are not
  part of the stable editor contract.
- Auto-publishing depends on the connected account supporting video upload.

Until these areas are complete, treat the UI as a template-specific editor,
not a promise that every combination can render and publish successfully.
