## Outcome

You clone the **Study Tips** catalog template into a user-owned automation,
adapt it for finals preparation, generate an unpublished slideshow, review the
result, and export its PNG files. The shared catalog template is never modified.

This workflow was verified in Chrome on July 17, 2026. The completed run created
**4 Active Recall Mistakes Before Finals**, with six slides, no scheduled date,
and **Not published** status.

## Before you start

- Confirm **Study Tips** appears under **Start from a proven workflow** on Home
  or in the **New Automation** template picker.
- Keep the included **Pinterest - UGC Laptop Study** collection unless you have
  a better captioned collection of students, notes, desks, and revision scenes.
- Use the template as a structural starting point. Replace hooks and directions
  that do not match the narrower finals topic.

## 1. Choose the Study Tips template

1. Open **Home**.
2. Under **Start from a proven workflow**, find **Study Tips**.
3. Review its example covers if needed, then select **Use**.

![Home page with the Study Tips quick-start template and Use action](/docs/workflows/study-template-01-home.jpg)

_Use creates an editable copy; it does not change the catalog template._

## 2. Rename the cloned automation

1. Wait for the **Study Tips** automation overview to open.
2. Select the edit icon beside its name.
3. Rename it to **Finals Study Tips** and press **Enter**.

![Cloned Study Tips automation with inherited hooks and settings](/docs/workflows/study-template-02-cloned.jpg)

_The clone inherited 20 hooks and the complete slideshow format before any customization._

## 3. Inspect the inherited slideshow format

1. Open **Slideshow Format**.
2. Confirm the template already provides:
   - one hook slide;
   - five content slides;
   - two text elements on each content slide;
   - the **Pinterest - UGC Laptop Study** collection.
3. Do not rebuild the layout when the inherited structure already fits the
   requested list format.

![Inherited Study Tips slideshow structure in the format editor](/docs/workflows/study-template-03-format.jpg)

_A template is useful because its layout, text hierarchy, media collection, and pacing arrive together._

## 4. Confirm the content structure and media

1. Open **Content**.
2. Keep **Slide count** at `5`.
3. Confirm the content collection is **Pinterest - UGC Laptop Study**.
4. Enable **AI image matching** only when its captions are accurate enough to
   distinguish notes, desks, laptops, and study actions.

![Study Tips content section with five slides and its inherited study collection](/docs/workflows/study-template-04-content.jpg)

_The verified generation used the inherited collection without importing new media._

## 5. Adapt the two content directions

Each repeated content slide contains a short heading and a supporting
explanation.

1. Select the first **Edit text element** action on a content slide and change
   **Content direction** to:

   ```text
   numbered finals study habit or practical exam-prep tactic, 5-10 words, all
   lowercase
   ```

2. Select the second text element and change its direction to:

   ```text
   specific reason or action explaining how to apply the finals tactic this
   week, 15-25 words, all lowercase, no unsupported performance claims
   ```

3. Select **Save Changes**.

![Content text editor adapted from general study advice to finals preparation](/docs/workflows/study-template-05-directions.jpg)

_Editing the repeated template elements updates the role of every generated content slide._

## 6. Replace broad inherited hooks

1. Open **Hooks & Style**, then **Hooks**.
2. Replace the 20 general study hooks with a focused set:

   ```text
   5 finals study habits that reduce last-minute cramming
   4 active recall mistakes to fix before finals
   5 ways to make your finals study plan realistic
   3 revision habits to start one week before exams
   5 signs your study routine needs a reset
   ```

3. Keep **all lowercase** casing to match the inherited visual identity.

![Hooks editor containing five finals-specific study hooks](/docs/workflows/study-template-06-hooks.jpg)

_A smaller coherent hook set makes random hook selection stay within the same audience promise._

## 7. Adapt the inherited writing style

1. Open **Style** and keep **Custom** tone.
2. Replace **Custom tone** with:

   ```text
   Use a calm, practical coaching voice in second person. Keep all text
   lowercase and easy to scan. Be encouraging without shaming students or
   promising guaranteed grades.
   ```

3. Replace **Slideshow writing style** with:

   ```text
   Give one specific finals-preparation action per slide. Pair a numbered 5-10
   word heading with a 15-25 word explanation a student can apply this week.
   Avoid vague motivation, invented statistics, and guaranteed outcomes.
   ```

4. Select **Save Changes**.

![Study Tips custom tone and slideshow style adapted for finals](/docs/workflows/study-template-07-style.jpg)

_The edit preserves the template's calm educational identity while removing grade guarantees and generic advice._

## 8. Confirm the owned automation is ready

Return to **Overview** and confirm:

- the name is **Finals Study Tips**;
- **Hooks & Style** reports five hooks;
- no output has been generated yet;
- no manual publication date is present.

![Customized Finals Study Tips automation ready for its first generation](/docs/workflows/study-template-08-ready.jpg)

_The original Study Tips catalog entry remains available for other automations._

## 9. Generate an unpublished draft

1. Select **Generate**.
2. Confirm the result card displays **Generating**.
3. Wait for generation to finish; do not navigate away and assume success from
   the initial card.

![Finals Study Tips result card during generation](/docs/workflows/study-template-09-generating.jpg)

_The verified run took longer than the blank astrology example but completed normally._

## 10. Confirm the completed result

The generated result should display **Not published**, a creation time, and no
publication time. The verified run selected the hook **4 active recall mistakes
to fix before finals**.

![Completed Study Tips result titled 4 Active Recall Mistakes Before Finals](/docs/workflows/study-template-10-ready.jpg)

_A manual template run remains unpublished and unscheduled even if the automation later receives a schedule._

## 11. Review every generated slide

1. Open the completed result.
2. Check the hook and each numbered mistake in order.
3. Confirm the text remains lowercase and the examples are actionable.
4. Reject unsupported grade promises, repeated advice, irrelevant media, or a
   hook count that does not match its payoff.
5. Review the generated title, caption, and hashtags.

![Generated Study Tips slideshow with its hook, first mistake, and publishing details](/docs/workflows/study-template-11-review.jpg)

_The verified output used six slides: hook, four mistakes, and a final action slide._

## 12. Export the reviewed slideshow

1. Inspect the final slide and confirm it closes the list with a useful next
   action.
2. Select **Export PNGs**.
3. Confirm the download is created. The verified export was
   `4-active-recall-mistakes-before-finals.zip`.

![Final action slide in the generated Study Tips slideshow](/docs/workflows/study-template-12-final.jpg)

_Exporting creates the posting files but does not publish or mark the output as published._

## Success check

- **Study Tips** was cloned with **Use**, not edited in the catalog.
- The owned copy was renamed and narrowed to finals preparation.
- Its collection, format, and visual hierarchy were inherited successfully.
- Its hooks and text directions were deliberately customized.
- The generated output contains a paid-off hook and coherent study advice.
- The result remains **Not published** and has no publication date.
- **Export PNGs** produces a ZIP containing the final slideshow images.
